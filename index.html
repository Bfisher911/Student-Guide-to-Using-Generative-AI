A Student’s Guide to Using Generative AI

(Version 2.0 – June 19, 2025)

0 Quick-Start Card

Do

  • ✓ Use AI for brainstorming, explaining tough ideas, or polishing draft wording.
  • ✓ Check every fact, citation, code snippet, or translation an AI gives you.
  • ✓ Add a short disclosure + citation whenever AI meaningfully shapes your submission.
  • ✓ Use institution-approved tools or turn off “chat-history & training” in public models.

Don’t

  • ✗ Paste exam questions or entire assignments into AI during closed-book tests.
  • ✗ Submit raw AI output as “your” work.
  • ✗ Hide your AI use or claim it as wholly original.
  • ✗ Upload confidential, personal (PII), or unpublished research data.

1 Guiding Principles for AI Use

Many academic institutions now let students use AI to augment learning, but insist on integrity, transparency, and privacy. The policies here reflect common guard-rails adopted at major universities.

2 The Four Golden Rules

3 Allowed vs. Prohibited Uses

Allowed (with disclosure)

  • Brainstorming ideas, outlines, or research questions.
  • Getting plain-language explanations of complex concepts.
  • Generating study flashcards or self-quizzes.
  • Grammar and style improvements (similar to Grammarly).
  • Debugging code when the instructor explicitly permits.

Prohibited

  • Using AI during closed-book quizzes/exams.
  • Paraphrasing to dodge plagiarism checkers.
  • Uploading sensitive or confidential institutional data.
  • Submitting AI-invented references or unverified data.
  • Delegating all parts of a graded task to AI.
Heads Up! Your course syllabus may narrow or expand what counts as "Allowed." Always check first.

4 Incidental vs. Substantive Use

Incidental Use

Zero content from the model ends up in the final work (e.g., “explain quantum tunnelling so I can understand the reading”). No disclosure needed.

Substantive Use

Any AI text, ideas, code, images, or structure appear in the submission. Must be disclosed & cited.

5 Using AI the Right Way

1. Plan

Decide which part of the task AI will assist (outline? example code?).

2. Prompt

Ask clear, specific questions; avoid sensitive data.

3. Verify

Fact-check; run plagiarism or code linters; replace hallucinated references.

4. Transform

Rewrite in your own voice; add analysis, citations, and course concepts.

5. Disclose & Cite

See templates below. This is a critical step for academic integrity.

6. Reflect

Did AI actually help you learn? If not, adjust your approach next time.
